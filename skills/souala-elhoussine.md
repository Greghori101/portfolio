# SOUALA ELHOUSSINE – Biography

My name is Souala Elhoussine. I am a Computer Systems Engineer, Software Engineer, and currently a PhD researcher in Quantum Computing.

My journey started at the Higher School of Computer Science of Sidi Bel Abbès (ESI-SBA), where I studied from 2019 to 2024. Throughout my studies, I was always passionate about mathematics and physics. These were not simply subjects I had to study; they were the disciplines that attracted me the most and where I consistently performed well. My transcripts reflect this strong foundation, especially in mathematical analysis, algebra, probability, statistics, algorithms, and computer architecture.

The first two years at ESI-SBA were highly theoretical. We studied mathematics, algorithms, data structures, computer architecture, operating systems, electronics, probability, statistics, logic, and physics. This period built the analytical mindset that would later shape my engineering and research career.

During my second year, we completed a team project to build an examination planning system using PHP and JavaScript. Working in a team of six students taught me the importance of collaboration, communication, and software development practices. In addition to this project, every module involved mini-projects, reports, and practical work, including networking and Linux-related tasks.

At the end of the preparatory cycle, students had to pass a competitive national examination to gain access to the engineering cycle. I successfully passed this examination and ranked among the top ten candidates, allowing me to continue my engineering studies.

My third year focused heavily on core computer science disciplines. We studied networking, cryptography, probability, statistics, databases, operating systems, and software engineering. During this period, I worked on a project designed to manage academic projects within ESI-SBA, allowing students and supervisors to track progress and coordinate project activities.

In the fourth year, students had to choose among three specializations: Artificial Intelligence, Web Technologies, and Computer Systems Engineering (ISI). I chose Computer Systems Engineering because I was interested in systems, networking, security, software architecture, and distributed applications.

During this year, we studied advanced algorithms, web development, mobile development, UML, software design, project management, networking, cybersecurity, and introductory artificial intelligence. One of my major projects was the design of a complete clinical information system. The project included network architecture, security planning, infrastructure design, and software development for managing patients, doctors, appointments, administrative processes, and medical information.

The fifth year was divided into two complementary tracks: the engineering project and the master's research project.

For my master's research project, I worked on object detection using deep learning for automotive spare parts. The goal was to develop intelligent computer vision models capable of recognizing spare parts automatically.

For my engineering project, I developed AutoHub, a startup-oriented platform composed of three main components. The first component was an automotive spare parts marketplace. The second was an intelligent camera system that allowed users to search for spare parts through image recognition. The third was an Uber-like towing service integrated with mechanic management and customer communication features.

Alongside my studies, I completed several internships. My first internship was at Sonelgaz during my third year, where I spent one month working on IT and infrastructure-related activities. During my fourth year, I completed a second internship at Sonelgaz that lasted two months and provided additional exposure to enterprise information systems and technical operations.

For my final graduation internship, I joined the Computer Science Research Laboratory of Sidi Bel Abbès (LabRI-SBA) for six months. This experience introduced me to scientific research methodologies, literature reviews, experimentation, and academic research environments. It was my first serious exposure to research and played a major role in shaping my future academic ambitions.

After graduation, I entered the software industry. In July 2024, I joined Apollo Digital Solutions as a Software Engineer. For approximately one year and two months, I worked on modern web applications, backend systems, APIs, cloud deployments, software architecture, and production-grade systems. This experience significantly improved my engineering skills and exposed me to real-world software development challenges.

Later, I joined SobiAPI as a Backend Engineer, where I focused on backend development, APIs, databases, and server-side architecture.

Although I enjoyed software engineering, I realized that my long-term ambition was research. I decided to return to academia and pursue a career in quantum computing.

I participated in the highly competitive national doctoral entrance examination in Quantum Computing. More than 3000 candidates participated across different specializations and universities. The examination consisted of two major parts. The first focused on algorithms and data structures. The second, with a higher coefficient, covered quantum computing, databases, networking, and security.

I ranked first among candidates coming from quantum computing master's programs and secured admission to the doctoral program. Ultimately, only 51 candidates were selected nationally from seven universities.

Today, I am a PhD researcher working on Quantum-Safe Blockchain and Distributed Ledger Technologies. My research interests include quantum algorithms, quantum optimization, quantum error correction, fault-tolerant quantum computing, post-quantum cryptography, distributed systems, and blockchain technologies.

To strengthen my expertise, I completed courses on quantum error correction and actively study Qiskit, quantum algorithms, and IBM Quantum systems. I regularly experiment on IBM Quantum Cloud and continue exploring the practical aspects of quantum computing.

I have also participated in multiple quantum computing hackathons, where I worked on implementing research papers, conducting literature reviews, and solving optimization and NP-hard problems using quantum approaches.

Currently, I am preparing my first scientific publication as part of my doctoral research.

Beyond my academic and professional activities, I enjoy building practical projects. Some of the projects I have developed include a school management system, a fire detection device optimized for large-scale deployment in forests, a marine radio mesh communication network designed to improve resource distribution between sea and land operations, multiple dashboards and platforms for clients and freelancers, and a medical image annotation platform inspired by systems such as IMAIOS for labeling and annotation workflows.

My long-term goal is to contribute to the advancement of quantum computing, particularly in quantum algorithms, optimization, fault-tolerant architectures, and secure distributed systems, while combining my background in software engineering, mathematics, and computer science research.
